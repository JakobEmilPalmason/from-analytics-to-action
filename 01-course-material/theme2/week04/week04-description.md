### Week 4 (23 Feb) - Datafication

In this section of the course, we will focus on what it means in practice to make data and data analysis valuable to different stakeholders in an organization. The three modules in the section deal first with questions about datafication (today's topic), then how to discover relevant questions through exploratory data visualization, and finally how to involve stakeholders through participatory data design. Each module will end with a practical prompt for how to develop your presentation to the case companies. 

Datafication refers to the processes through which data is produced, the interests that different stakeholders can have in this process, and the consequences the choices made can have for data analysis down the line. Why do we have the data we have? Whose interests does it serve? What does it allow us to know (and what does it not allow us to know)? And could it be different? 

Through a short review of the literature, Mejias and Couldry provides a relatively easy to read introduction to the concept of datafication and its ramifications. It is advisable to start here. Flyverbom and Madsen takes a broader look at how data is made valuable in organizations through two case examples. They develop a typology of valuation practices - four analytical moments - along the social life of data, from production to structuring, distribution, and visualization. It is particularly the early stages of this process we are interested in when we talk about datafication. Contrary to Mjias and Couldry, Flyverbom and Madsen provide concrete examples of datafication choices and their implications in the two cases they work with. This is likely where it will be easiest to relate directly to your own data projects.
