Time to reassess data value: The many faces of data in 
organizations (Xu et al., 2024) (2/2)

• Contributions:
– Developed typology that shows the diverse roles that data play in organizations,

demonstrating the distinctive characteristics associated with each role. 
– Discussion of how this typology can assist practitioners in leveraging this deeper

understanding as they seek to effectively monetize, manage, and govern data assets in 
their organizations

• Data exhibit varied characteristics depending on their usage in different 
business contexts

• Primary roles that data play in organizations: data as a tool, data as a 
commodity, data as a practice, and data as algorithmic intelligence

1. Data as tool (Xu et al., 2024)

• Data are used to support decision-making processes in managerial contexts

• Attention is directed to “instrumental effects” of data and its role in mediation 
between individuals and environment.

• “While using data is more of a human centred activity (Knox, 2007), the user and 
the organization can vary the needs and purpose of using data, reshape the way 
of use, and improve the outcome (Orlikowski and Iacono, 2001; Jones, 2019).” p. 
5

• In sum, data helps people make better decisions by turning information into 
managerial insight. Value comes through improving judgment, planning, and 
strategy.

2. Data as commodity (Xu et al., 2024)

• Data is traded or sold as goods to generate direct revenue or exchanged for 
other resources, mostly between organizations.

• Data trading emerges as the main scene; the value creation process, mostly 
inter-organizational, involves a two-sided market coordination between buyers 
and sellers

• Common characteristic is the attribution of a monetary value during 
transactions

3. Data as practice (Xu et al., 2024)

• Repeatable patterns of activities that leverage data for continuous 
improvements in business operations, and for optimizations of products or 
services

• Significance of data in driving digital innovations and facilitating 
organizational learning

• Ensemble view emphasizes the interactions and relationships between IT 
artifacts and the groups involved in their construction, implementation, and 
use

4. Data as algorithmic intelligence (Xu et al., 2024)

• Role of data in supporting process automation and algorithmic decision-
making, thereby generating value for organizations at an unprecedented pace 
and with remarkable versatility, potentially surpassing our imaginations. This is 
achieved through various forms of artificial intelligence (hereafter referred to as 
“AI”) and machine learning applications in business.

• The computational view: Technology can be represented here through 
algorithmic endeavours to build new or enhance existing computational 
systems that can support some human activity.

Implications for practice (Xu et al., 2024)

1. Data as tool: emphasis on individuals and that organizations develop their data

related skills and competencies (e.g., data literacy).

2. Data as comodity: Data should no longer been seen as a cost for

organizations but a source of profit. Data as most liquid assets that an 
organization possesses.

3. Data as practice: Importance of embedding data within a repeatable process

and formalizing it. People are just one part of this ensemble (e.g., centralized 
data platforms to democratize enterprise data to a broader audience).

4. Data as algorithmic intelligence: Potential of data in replacing human labour

and enhancing our ability in solving complex problems.
